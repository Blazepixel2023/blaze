Sunday at 9:50 AM SkyBlock - Year 300


Hello!

Today is the day SkyBlock reaches its 300th year, over 1500 real-life days since the game's inception. As has quickly become tradition, we will be celebrating the turn of the century with lots of cake! However, there will also be some special events taking place throughout the year for everyone to get involved in. Read on to find out more!

âº Century Cakes!
In celebration of such a momentous occasion, Simon himself has appeared in the Hub. As is typical of century celebrations, he will hand out a random Century Cake to each player who speaks to him. Not only can this be any of the 12 previously existing cakes, but some players may also receive one of two new cakes just added to the game! The Undead Century Cake grants those who consume it Vitality, and players who eat the Not-a-lie cake will find themselves given a small True Defense boost.


âº Raffles!
This year, Simon has brought a friend! Rezzus can be found at the Raffle Stand just in front of the Community Center in the Hub. Players who speak to him will receive a snazzy emblem showcasing which team they've been designated as part of the raffle event. Players who have been assigned a team will receive a raffle ticket for every 20 minutes spent online. Active playing can also reward players with a second ticket, which has a separate 20-minute cooldown.

All tickets a player collects will be automatically entered into the next raffle of each type. Raffles are held from the beginning of Year 300 until the end, and consist of three types:
  • The Speed Raffle is drawn every 2 hours, real-life time. All tickets players collected within the last two hours will be entered.​
  • The Daily Raffle is drawn every 24 hours. All tickets players collected within the last 24 hours will be entered. Due to there being more tickets entered, there will also be more rewards!​
  • The Big One is drawn once, at the end of Year 300. It gives the most (and best) rewards of any raffle type, and all tickets gathered throughout the entirety of Year 300 will be entered into the draw.​

All of this means that each ticket is entered into three different draws, giving you three separate chances at winning a prize! Fret not, however, as even the more unlucky entrants into the daily and big raffles will receive a consolation prize scaling with how many tickets they gathered.

âº Raffle Milestones
Another way in which those who participate in the event can earn rewards is through Raffle Milestones. As players gather tickets, they will receive rewards upon reaching certain thresholds. There is no raffle here - every player who reaches a milestone will be able to immediately claim the corresponding reward. With emblems, pet skins, and even a talisman up for grabs, nobody has to go home empty-handed!


âº A Special Memento
Remember when we spoke about teams being assigned earlier? This is where that comes into play. Throughout the event, a tally will be kept of how many raffle tickets each team has collected in total. By the end of the event, the team that has gathered the most tickets will be declared the winner. All players who took part in the event will then receive a special memento item, though only the winning team will be immortalized in the item's description.


And that's it! The Year 300 celebrations are live right now, so get online and have some fun! A massive thanks to the community for being so passionate about the game, and we look forward to celebrating again with you soon!

Friday at 1:55 PM Hypixel Tournaments - Wool Wars 4v4 (2nd iteration/rerun)


It's time to test your skill in our next Hypixel Tournament, Wool Wars 4v4!
If you don't know about tournaments or the Tournament Hall, or if you'd like to know more about some of the terms in this post, make sure to take a look at the first tournament post.

Due to the previous tournament last week being canceled, we are hosting a re-run/2nd iteration next weekend! If you missed the news about the previously canceled tournament, you can read more information here. To keep players up to date, we have made the following changes in the last week:
  • We've reduced the amount of playable games from 100 to 80 based on feedback from players.
  • We've made changes to improve the pregame-obfuscation system that should prevent methods to identify players in the server.
  • Reworked some of the matchmaking code to use a single queue for all tournaments going forward
    • This means for games such as Wool Wars that had a custom queue, if the mode is a tournament we will use our standard queue instead of this so we can be sure all tournaments have the same matchmaking and work as expected.
    • These changes have also allowed us to do some under-the-hood optimizations and add some additional metrics for monitoring the matchmaking results.
    • This change is also undergoing large internal testing with our QA team to validate there are no missed issues with making sure players are in their correct brackets.
  • We've rolled out a new system that works alongside game history to include tournament information, this includes the servers bracket and points earned for each player in the game.
    • While this wouldn't have allowed us to fix the previously canceled tournament for the same reasons previously mentioned, this gives both us and players an easy way to view what bracket servers they have played in and the points they earned.
  • We also released the participation rewards for the previously canceled iteration, if you haven't already claimed them you can still do so in the rewards NPC under the previous tournaments option.

The Wool Wars tournament will take place on September 8th.
It will last 48 hours total, from 6:00 PM EDT September 8 to 6:00 PM EDT September 10.

There will be a Tournament Mode NPC in the Wool Wars lobby (as well as the tournament Hall).
